Project Overview

VR Goggles for Seasonal Depression are virtual reality goggles that I designed for a school project. The end-user of the goggles are those who suffer from seasonal depression and are unable to participate in the activities that they most enjoy.  

Ideation Phase

During the initial phase of the project, my team and I designed around the goal of helping those with seasonal depression. We knew wood was a very common material found in nature, so I created designs around that concept.  

Physical Model

In the second phase, I modeled a mock pair of goggles using styrene and cardboard to highlight how the hinges in our project could work. This also helped identify issues with the design.


The final design of the goggles were much more secure than the first model. Using metal hinges and a closed design, I was able to create a more secure fit, which addressed the issues with the first model.

Challenges

As I was designing the VR goggles, there was one pressing issue: ergonomics. I spent countless iterations revising the comfort of the headset. I wanted to find ways to create a sturdy frame for the goggles, while not compromising on aesthetics and overall function. Another challenge was designing strong connecting joints. Joints that were designed too thin would snap. My solution was to brace thin joins against the main frame to relieve stresses and provide structure and security.  

Reflection

The Seasonal Depression VR goggles was my first experience designing a functional product. This was a stressful project with many factors to consider, the human form, weight, aesthetics, and function. I am really proud that I was able to come up with such a solution like a skeleton, it is one of my proudest accomplishments of ingenuity and creative thinking.
