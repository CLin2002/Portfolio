Project Overview

Pavilion In a Park is a school project where I was tasked to design a pavilion and pergola within the boundaries of Holland Park in Surrey, British Columbia. My team was tasked to design a pavilion and pergola for the purpose of music recitals. Due to the finite area of Holland Park, we had to carefully plan our design around the size restrictions.

Design Process

During the first phase of the design, each member of the team was responsible in designing a pavilion. While designing, we focused on symmetry as our main design language.


The second phase of our design was to actualize the pavilion in Rhinoceros3D. With the 3D elements actualized, it gave us a new sense of direction.


While in the final phase of the project, I was in charge of assigning materials to our design. Using TwinMotion, I had the choice to decide on the materiality of our project. Using stone and polished metal, I created a visually interesting pavilion by adding accenting colours. After assigning materials, I created a video using TwinMotion to capture the brilliance of our design.

Challenges

Due to our original design intent, symmetry, we faced the challenge of creating an interesting shape that was beyond basic geometry. We wanted a shape that was visually stunning and breath taking. 


Our solution to this issue was to wrap the pavilion with a more intricate shape. While the main structure of our pergola is at its core, a cylinder, the addition of external elements helps create visual interest.  

Reflection

Pavilion In a Park challenged my creativity while using real-world thinking to set restrictions. I am ecstatic that my initial design translated into the final product. More than just design, this project taught me about lighting, shadows, and materiality. I am glad that I was able to marry all three of those ideas into one cohesive piece of art and architecture.
